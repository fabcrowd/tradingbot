How does it work?

The bot acts like a market maker — the same role played by banks and professional trading desks on every exchange in the world. Instead of predicting whether XRP goes up or down, it profits from the gap between buyers and sellers.

THE CORE MECHANIC
Every asset on Kraken has two prices at any moment: the bid (what buyers will pay) and the ask (what sellers want). The gap between them is called the spread. The bot plants one order on each side of that gap, collects the difference when both fill, then does it again.

Here's a simplified example with XRP at $2.3450:

EXAMPLE CYCLE — XRP/USDT
Mid price$2.3450
Bot places BUY at$2.3441
Bot places SELL at$2.3459
Both orders fill✓
Spread captured+$0.0018 per XRP
On 30 XRP order size+$0.054 per cycle

That looks tiny. But run that cycle every 3 seconds across hundreds of fills per day and it compounds. The bot never sleeps, never gets emotional, and never chases a trade.

KEY INSIGHT
The bot doesn't care if XRP goes up or down. It profits from activity — the constant back-and-forth movement of price that happens every second of every day.

What settings can I change?

There are five core settings. Each one changes how aggressively or conservatively the bot operates.

SPREAD BPS
Default: 8 bps · Range: 1–200
How wide you set the gap between your buy and sell orders. 1 basis point = 0.01% of price. Tighter spread = more fills, smaller profit per fill. Wider spread = fewer fills, more profit per fill. Start at 8–12 until you understand the market rhythm.
ORDER SIZE
Default: 30 XRP · Adjust to your balance
How many units of crypto each order is for. Bigger size = bigger profit per cycle, but also bigger exposure if the market moves against you. A safe rule: order size should be no more than 5–10% of your total balance value.
MAX INVENTORY
Default: 300 XRP
The maximum amount of crypto the bot is allowed to accumulate. This is your safety valve — it stops the bot buying more if the market is only moving one direction. If inventory hits the limit, the bot pauses buying until it can sell some off.
CONFIG IN THIS REPO
Dashboard copy above is the idea. In config.toml, per pair: spread_bps is the half-spread per side (total quoted width ≈ 2×). Use order_size, max_inventory, and fee-aware fields from fee_schedule / spread_floor_bps so quotes stay profitable after Kraken maker fees.

How does profit scale?

Market-making P&L is roughly: (edge per round-trip × fill rate) minus fees, adverse selection, and inventory risk. More frequent two-sided fills with a spread that clears fees is what compounds—not “being right” about direction.

LEVER IN THIS CODEBASE
The learner can adjust half-spread within configured floors/ceilings; fee tiers improve as 30-day volume grows. When testing changes, compare net P&L and inventory stability, not raw fill count alone.
04 //
Is it safe?

No automated trading system is “safe” in the sense of guaranteed returns. You can reduce operational risk with paper mode, low order size, strict max inventory, and loss limits—then prove behavior on data before scaling size.

PRACTICAL CHECKLIST
Start small, watch data/trades_paper.jsonl (or live equivalent), keep API keys scoped, and never rely on a single lucky session as proof.
05 //
How can I lose?
↯
TREND / INVENTORY
One-way markets stack inventory against you. Max inventory and skew controls exist to cap that, but they do not remove directional risk.
%
FEES & SPREAD TOO TIGHT
If quoted spread does not cover fees and adverse selection, you can lose on every round-trip even with “lots of fills.”
⚡
TECH & EXCHANGE
Disconnects, partial fills, latency, and bugs can all create unintended exposure. Monitor logs and P&L stops if configured.
